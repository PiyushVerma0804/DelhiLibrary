About Libraries

Why Libraries Matter in a Changing World

Libraries are the living memory of civilisations. In Delhi — a city layered with millennia of history — public libraries serve as sanctuaries of thought, culture, and democratic learning. They are places where every citizen, regardless of background, can access the sum of human knowledge.

From the majestic halls of the Delhi Public Library to the specialised collections of India International Centre, these institutions preserve rare manuscripts, foster research, and cultivate the habit of reading in a digital age.

This guide celebrates Delhi's finest libraries — their heritage, their collections, and the communities that breathe life into them.

Curated Picks

Notable Libraries of Delhi

Four remarkable institutions that define Delhi's intellectual and cultural landscape.

Est. 1951

Delhi Public Library

Established in 1951 under UNESCO's pilot project, the Delhi Public Library is one of the oldest and largest public library networks in India. With over 35 branch libraries across the city, it houses millions of books in multiple languages including Hindi, Urdu, Sanskrit, and English — making knowledge truly accessible to all.

S.P. Mukherjee Marg, Daryaganj, New Delhi
Mon – Sat 9:00 AM – 7:00 PM

Est. 1962

India International Centre Library

The IIC Library is a celebrated intellectual hub nestled in the heart of Lodhi Estate. Known for its curated collection of books on international affairs, arts, culture, and the social sciences, it attracts academics, diplomats, and scholars from across the globe. The reading rooms offer a serene atmosphere unmatched in the city.

40, Max Mueller Marg, Lodhi Estate, New Delhi
Mon – Sun 9:30 AM – 8:00 PM
Read More
Historical

Parliament Library

One of India's most prestigious libraries, the Parliament Library (also known as the Library of Parliament) serves Members of Parliament with comprehensive research support. Its vast collection spans law, governance, political science, economics, and history, backed by a team of expert reference librarians and a state-of-the-art digital archive system.

Parliament House, Sansad Marg, New Delhi
By Appointment Official Access Only

Est. 1862

Hardayal Municipal Public Library

Established in 1862 during the British Raj, the Hardayal Municipal Public Library (formerly Delhi Public Library at Chandni Chowk) is one of the oldest libraries in the national capital. It houses thousands of rare books, Hindi and Urdu periodicals, and historical documents, offering free membership to residents of Delhi — a true gem of Old Delhi.

Chandni Chowk, Old Delhi, New Delhi – 110006
Mon – Sat 8:00 AM – 8:00 PM
